Final Summary——
Traffic system for Canada Geese in the city:

intro:
For the final project, I will be teaming up with Karen El Asmar. After a in-depth field around NYC, we found that bees and other animals, which we chose as research objects, can hardly be found in the city. So we decided to keep working on the Canada Geese in the city, as an continuation work of our midterm project.

Summary of Week 1:

After our week 1-4 research, we realized that the problems that exist with our first prototype and the main issues we need to address are the following:
1. Geese can get used to an easy food source
2. We need a more sustainable approach
3. We need to find different/ more natural ways of communicating a signal.

Summary of Week 2:

We focus on exploring more natural ways of communication

We decided to work on repel vs. attract systems. We focused on 3 systems:
1. Eye spot
2. Reflective Surface
3. Sound

Summary of Week 3:

We focus on exploring more natural ways of communication. We decided to work on repel vs. attract systems. We focused on 3 systems:
1. Eye spot
2. Reflective Surface
3. Sound

The first two experiments proved to us that geese are not attracted or repelled by any of these systems. We believe that these systems might work in more rural areas, however, for city geese, these systems proved to be unefficient.

Using sound seemed to be very effective and we got a lot of responses from these geese. Sounds we used were the Canada Geese soundtracks from the Cornell Lab of Ornithology. The problem we found first was that we were using the sounds randomly and these geese responded to the aggressive sounds as well as to the mating sounds.

Hence, for the sound tests:
1. We got a response
2. Geese were alert
3. Geese cannot detect sound source

We also found that the only thing that lures these geese is food.

Summary of Week 4:

We focus on combining Sound & Form for Geese & Humans. After these tests and our findings, for this week, we decided to combine both sound and form for both geese and human.

Main Purpose: Design a set of devices whose ultimate goal is to establish a harmonious living between geese and humans in an urban context.

Goals: To help both city dwellers co-exist, For better communication between geese and humans,  To keep geese safe,  Educate human city dwellers about geese.  

Final testing: test the usability of the devices.
